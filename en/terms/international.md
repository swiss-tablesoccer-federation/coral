## T&C for Member Associations

### English — Member Associations

```
T&C for Member Associations

The following General Terms and Conditions govern the rights and obligations of member associations towards the Swiss Tablesoccer Federation (STF).

1. As a member of Swiss Olympic, the STF is subject to the Ethics Charter (https://www.swissolympic.ch/athlete-hub/Pers-nlichkeit-und-Ethik/werte-ethik/ethik-charta), the Ethics Statute (https://www.swissolympic.ch/athlete-hub/Pers-nlichkeit-und-Ethik/werte-ethik/Ethik-Statut-des-Schweizer-Sports) and the Doping Statute (https://www.swissolympic.ch/verbaende/praevention/antidoping) of Swiss Olympic as well as further clarifying documents.

2. The Ethics Charter, the Ethics Statute and the Doping Statute as well as further clarifying documents are binding for the STF itself, its employees, board members, members, sub-organisations (e.g. partial, regional or cantonal associations, sections, clubs) as well as their respective bodies, members, employees, athletes, coaches, support staff, doctors and officials.

3. The STF is committed to healthy, respectful, fair and successful sport. It embodies these values by treating others with respect, acting and communicating transparently – both itself and its bodies and members. To this end, the STF and its direct and indirect members acknowledge and comply with the Ethics Charter, the Ethics Statute of Swiss Sport and the Doping Statute of Swiss Olympic as well as further clarifying documents. The sports federation disseminates these principles within its sphere of activity.

4. As members of the STF, clubs and their members are subject to the Ethics Charter, the Ethics Statute and the Doping Statute of Swiss Olympic as well as further clarifying documents.

5. Member associations commit to complying with the regulations and statutes of the STF and to actively contributing to their implementation.

6. Member associations are obliged to inform their members about the applicable rules and obligations and to ensure compliance.

7. The STF aims to protect the integrity, safety and fairness of table soccer competitions from any form of manipulation and/or corrupt activities.

8. Member associations and their members are obliged to refrain from any form of unfair influence and manipulation of sporting competitions, and in particular to comply with the relevant provisions of the ITSF (International Table Soccer Federation) and the Ethics Statute (https://www.swissolympic.ch/athlete-hub/Pers-nlichkeit-und-Ethik/werte-ethik/Ethik-Statut-des-Schweizer-Sports) of Swiss Olympic. The provisions of the ITSF and the Ethics Statute for Swiss sport apply in all other respects.

9. In the event of violations of these T&C or the STF regulations, the STF reserves the right to take appropriate measures in accordance with the applicable statutes and regulations, including withdrawal of membership.
```
